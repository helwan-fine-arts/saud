Philosophy, aim, and importance of the Sustainable Architecture and Urban Design program

The aim of this program is driven by the Sustainable development goals (SDGs) and the importance of creating sustainable cities and communities. The Sustainable Architecture and Urban Design (SAUD) program at the faculty of Fine Arts, Helwan University, aims at preparing architects with the basics of both engineering and art specialized in sustainable architecture and urban design. The program introduces students to architectural and urban sciences and enables them to conceive sustainable architecture and urbanism concepts. Graduates can understand the relationship between societies, buildings, and the environment. Furthermore, graduates of SAUD program deal with complex architectural and urban environment problems on the city, urban and architectural levels.

The duration of studying architecture, as one of the specifications of Fine arts, is five years. It comprises all the architecture requirements as one of the specifications in the engineering and art sectors.

The Program Mission: The program aims to prepare an architect specialized in sustainable architecture and urban design who is aware of building sciences and related architectural and urban studies. It also aims at preparing an architect who uses the latest technologies and is committed to good behavior and professional ethics.

The Program Vision: It introduces advanced educational services linked to Egypt’s vision and is part of the current trends of developing architectural education.

The general skills that are acquired by the student in the architecture department in the faculty of Fine arts, Helwan university

  • Internationalization skills.
  • Entrepreneurial skills.
  • Professional skills.
  • Inter-personal skills
  • Personal skills.

The student acquires these skills through the transformation from education to learning, where the student becomes the core of the educational process. The student becomes more aware of self-learning and how to be the principal researcher for the information. The teaching staff should be continuously trained on making the learning process the main feature of studying in the department.
